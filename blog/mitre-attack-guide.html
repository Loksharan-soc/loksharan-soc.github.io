Understanding the MITRE ATT&CK Framework: A Guide for Aspiring SOC Analysts

Published: June 2025 | By Loksharan Saravanan

The MITRE ATT&CK Framework has become an essential tool for cybersecurity professionals, especially those working in Security Operations Centers (SOC) and threat hunting. In this guide, I’ll explain what the MITRE ATT&CK framework is, why it’s important, and how I’ve applied it in my labs and real-world projects to improve detection and response capabilities.

What is the MITRE ATT&CK Framework?

MITRE ATT&CK (Adversarial Tactics, Techniques, and Common Knowledge) is a globally-accessible knowledge base of adversary tactics and techniques based on real-world observations. It helps cybersecurity teams understand how attackers operate and provides a common language for describing their behavior.

"MITRE ATT&CK provides a matrix of tactics (the goals of an attacker) and techniques (the methods they use) to help defenders identify, detect, and respond to threats more effectively."

Why is MITRE ATT&CK Important?

By mapping cyber attacks to specific tactics and techniques in the ATT&CK framework, SOC analysts can:

  • Understand attacker behavior and anticipate their next moves.
  • Develop better detection rules and alerting mechanisms.
  • Prioritize defensive efforts based on attacker methods used against your environment.
  • Communicate findings clearly with a standardized vocabulary.

Applying MITRE ATT&CK in My Labs

In my Wazuh detection lab and Velociraptor threat hunting projects, I use MITRE ATT&CK to:

  • Map simulated attacks (e.g., brute force, PowerShell malware) to ATT&CK techniques like T1110 - Brute Force and T1059.001 - PowerShell.
  • Write detection rules that specifically target behaviors outlined in ATT&CK.
  • Document alerts and findings with ATT&CK technique IDs to maintain clarity and professional standards.

Example: Brute Force Attack Detection

When simulating brute force login attempts in my lab, I referenced MITRE ATT&CK technique T1110 to understand common attacker methods. This guided me to create Wazuh rules that detect multiple failed login attempts in a short timeframe, improving alert quality and reducing false positives.

Getting Started with MITRE ATT&CK

If you’re new to MITRE ATT&CK, I recommend:

  • Exploring the official ATT&CK website: attack.mitre.org
  • Starting with the Enterprise Matrix to understand common attacker tactics and techniques.
  • Using ATT&CK IDs when documenting your projects and detections.
  • Integrating ATT&CK mapping into your threat hunting workflows.

Conclusion

Understanding and applying the MITRE ATT&CK framework is a key skill for aspiring SOC analysts and cybersecurity professionals. It improves your ability to detect, analyze, and respond to threats with precision and professionalism.

As you build your labs and hands-on projects, make ATT&CK a central part of your methodology — it will set you apart in the job market and in real-world SOC environments.
